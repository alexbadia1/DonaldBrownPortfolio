Why I Am Running In April of 2018 I decided to retire from the practice of law. The decision was not an easy one; but it was 4-5 years in coming. I kept my license in case I could use it one day to be of service. I was asked by the local democratic committee to run for Town of Poughkeepsie Justice. After much consideration I decided that this is what I am called to do right now. I know the job. I know I have the best qualifications for the job and I pledge my full commitment, to the citizens of the Town of Poughkeepsie to do the job right and to give it all the time and energy it takes”.

Why Am I Qualified?

              I am a former Judge and a former prosecutor. As a prosecutor, I handled all criminal cases in the Town of Poughkeepsie Court, from shoplifting to murder.
I am the candidate who:

  • Was President of the Dutchess County Bar Assn
  • Was Chairman of the Judiciary Comm. of the Dutchess Co. Bar Assn. for 10 yrs.
  • Was tried well over 200 civil and criminal jury cases in state and federal courts
  • Has been recognized for extraordinary pro bono services to families of victims off Sept. 11, 2001 disaster at the World Trade Center.
  • Served for more than 4 years as Probation Officer and Sr. Investigator in the Dutchess County Probation Dept. and the Office of Probation of the City of N.Y
  • Was admitted to practice before the United State Supreme Court since 1984
  • Has published legal articles and lectured lawyers on Trial Practice issues for the NYS Bar Assn
  • Was a Faculty Member in Criminal Law at Marist College for 5 years
  • Received a ranking as a “Super Lawyer” based upon peer recommendations each year over the past 15 years
  • 40-year Member, NYS Magistrate’s Assn.

              For the past 20 years, I have received the highest rating from Judges and other attorneys from the Martindale-Hubbell rating agency which is the only truly independent, confidential peer review system in the country for over 70+ years. It’s highest rating (AV) is based upon Ethics and Legal Ability. Less than 3% of all attorneys in the U.S. have ever received the AV rating (which is updated every 2 years). I am most proud of this AV recognition.
              Perhaps the reason that I am best qualified for the job of Town Justice in Poughkeepsie, aside from the fact that I’ve lived here for most of the past 50 years, attended college here, coached Little League and practiced law here, is that I’ve stood in the shoes of every person who comes before the Town. I am a former Town Judge (in Millbrook, where I lived for 5 years after I left the D.A.’s Office), a former Asst. D.A. in the Town of Poughkeepsie, working 50-60 hours a week with the Judges, court personnel and, particularly, the Town Police Department. I spent 4 years in Probation, working a year in Dutchess Co. and then 3 ½ years working 4 p.m. to midnights in the Criminal Courts in Brooklyn and Manhattan for the NYC Office of Probation while attending law school days. I’ve represented clients before the Town Court from time to time over the years, as well. I have even been a defendant in a Town Court. In 1966, in my sophomore year at Marist, I received a ticket for a traffic infraction and the judge imposed a sentence of “$25 or 3 days”. In 1966, I had a part-time job which paid a lot less than $25 a month; so I asked for a week so that I could call my parents to ask them to send me the fine money. He refused the request and sent me off to 3 days in the Dutchess County Jail. (If you doubt this, just check out the mug shot on this site which I later retrieved with the help of a detective friend from the Sheriff’s Office years, later during my DA days).
              The point, of course, is that the job of Town Justice requires someone with some patience, wisdom and good judgment (which that certain unnamed, and now long- deceased jurist, lacked). The job I am running for is not a “big job”; but it is an important job. Important to the people who have need to come into or before that court.
              Finally, my best qualification may well be that I am not a politician. I did not seek this job. I was asked. I did not always want to be the Town Justice in the Town of Poughkeepsie. I retired from my law practice after 42 years, last April. I retained my law license in the event that I might use it one day to be of service in some way. I have been fortunate to have enjoyed a long and successful career and my career is over. I am no politico. I am interested only in being of some useful service to my family, friends and community. I seek no other job (political or otherwise). I seek no particular status. I am beholden to no party nor individuals. I seek only to do the job the way it should be done. I know the job quite well and how it should be done. I know that it is the busiest part-time court between White Plains and Albany. After my family, it will be my priority. it is a part time job and I will give it a a full-time commitment.
